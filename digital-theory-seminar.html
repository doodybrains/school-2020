weiner is attempting to now differentiate between closed, clockwork machines and modern automated machines. the former having no communication with the outside world and only existing and responding the messages from a pre-set pattern embedded within its machine. i suppose for simondon this would be an abstract machine of sorts in which each part of the machine serves a single function. weiner argues that more automated machines, such as the automatic door opener, unlike closed, clockwork machines, posess sense organs or receptors that are build to receive messages from external inputs. and like animals, which posess the ability to keep a record of the effects of the external enviroment on their beings, machines should also be able to bake in the effects of the external environment, so that they can use that sensory memory to respond accordingly.
